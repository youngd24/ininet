VCFMW INITECH Internet Netiquette Guide
Practice Good Netiquette!

Here are some basic rules to help you be a courteous member of this community:

  • Respect others: Treat online interactions with the same respect and consideration you would in face-to-face interactions.
  • Be mindful of your language: Avoid aggressive language, insults, or threats, and steer clear of "flaming," which is inflammatory or antagonistic criticism.
  • Don't "shout": Using all capital letters in online communication was considered rude and the equivalent of yelling.
  • Provide context: When replying to messages or participating in discussions, include enough of the original message to provide context for your response.
  • Be cautious with humor and sarcasm: Without the benefit of facial expressions or tone of voice, jokes, sarcasm, and dark humor could easily be misunderstood.
  • Avoid oversharing: Be careful about sharing personal information, as it could be used for identity theft or harassment.
  • Respect privacy: Don't share someone else's private information or photos without their permission.
  • Don't spam: Sending unsolicited bulk email or overloading someone's inbox with messages was considered poor netiquette.
  • Help newcomers: Be patient and helpful to those new to online interactions.
  • Think before posting: Before sending a message or posting content, take a moment to consider whether it's something you would be comfortable saying in person.
  • Avoid unnecessary email signatures: In the early days of the internet, long email signatures consumed precious processing time for the recipient, so keeping them short (under four lines) was considered good practice.
  • Be patient with responses: Don't expect immediate replies to emails, especially with time zone differences.
